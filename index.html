~~~~~Fasting~~~~~

I begin in the name of Allah, the Most Gracious and the Most Merciful (بسم الله الرحمن الرحيم)
Fasting is one of the five pillars of Islam. Fasting was made obligatory for Muslims in the second year of the Hijra. The month of Ramadan is a blessed and blessed month for Muslims, and all Muslims fast during this blessed month. The lexical meaning of the word “fasting” means “to abstain from something, to refrain from something, to be careful.” The meaning of fasting in Sharia is “to abstain from things that break the fast, namely, eating and drinking and sexual intercourse with the wife of one’s marriage, with the specific intention of those who have reached puberty, from dawn to sunset.” Fasting during the month of Ramadan is obligatory for every adult, sane, and settled Muslim. Young children, the sick, and travelers do not fast. However, travelers are advised to fast. Also, women who are menstruating or bleeding from childbirth do not fast until their bleeding stops. After Ramadan, they make up the days of fasting. Fasting reminds Muslims of Allah Almighty and awakens their responsibility. Allah Almighty has made fasting obligatory for humans to purify their inner world, to be characterized by good character, to have good relations with others, and to be pious. By fasting, a Muslim protects his eyes from looking at forbidden things, his ears from hearing forbidden things, his tongue from speaking falsely, his stomach from eating forbidden food, his hands from doing wrong things, and his feet from going to forbidden places. He always feels that Allah Almighty is watching and knowing.

Fasting is obligatory

Allah Almighty says about fasting in the Holy Qur'an: قال الله تعالي: » يَا أَيُّهَا الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا كُتِبَ عَلَيْكُمُ الصِّيَامُ كَمَا كُتِبَ عَلَى الَّذِينَ مِن قَبْلِكُمْ لَعَلَّكُمْ تَتَّقُون أَيَّامًا مَّعْدُودَاتٍ فَمَن كَانَ مِنكُم مَّرِيضًا أَوْ عَلَىٰ سَفَرٍ فَعِدَّةٌ مِّنْ أَيَّامٍ أُخَرَ وَعَلَى الَّذِينَ يُطِيقُونَهُ فِدْيَةٌ طَعَامُ مِسْكِينٍ فَمَن تَطَوَّعَ خَيْرًا فَهُوَ خَيْرٌ لَّهُ ۚ وَأَن تَصُومُوا خَيْرٌ لَّكُمْ ۖ إِن كُنتُمْ تَعْلَمُونَ شَهْرُ رَمَضَانَ الَّذِي أُنزِلَ فِيهِ الْقُرْآنُ هُدًى لِّلنَّاسِ وَبَيِّنَاتٍ مِّنَ الْهُدَىٰ وَالْفُرْقَانِ فَمَن شَهِدَ مِنكُمُ الشَّهْرَ فَلْيَصُمْهُ وَمَن كَانَ مَرِيضًا أَوْ عَلَىٰ سَفَرٍ فَعِدَّةٌ مِّنْ أَيَّامٍ أُخَرَ يُرِيدُ اللَّهُ بِكُمُ الْيُسْرَ وَلَا يُرِيدُ بِكُمُ الْعُسْرَ وَلِتُكْمِلُوا الْعِدَّةَ وَلِتُكَبِّرُوا اللَّهَ عَلَىٰ مَا هَدَاكُمْ وَلَعَلَّكُمْ تَشْكُرُونَ (البقرة(183 -185)
