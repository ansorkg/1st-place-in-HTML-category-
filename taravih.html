```About Tarawih```

Tarawih prayer is a 20-rak'ah sunnah prayer performed after the Isha prayer before the Witr prayer only during the month of Ramadan. Tarawih prayer is a sunnah act that must be performed by both men and women. Performing Tarawih prayer in congregation is sunnah. Tarawih prayer can be performed individually. However, it is preferable to perform it in congregation.

Our Prophet Muhammad (PBUH) said: “Whoever prays Tarawih with firm faith and hoping for a reward from Allah, his previous sins will be forgiven.” (Muslim, Salatul-Musafirin 174) – emphasizing the virtue of Tarawih, called for performing Tarawih.

Tarawih prayer consists of twenty rak'ahs. It is called "Tarawih" because there is a pause of one sa'am after every four rak'ahs of prayer. "Tarawih" means rest, relaxation, and repose. There are five pauses in a single Tarawih prayer. It is recommended to remember Allah, glorify Him, or send blessings upon our Prophet Muhammad during the break.

The intention for Tarawih prayer is: “I intend to perform Tarawih prayer for the sake of Allah, I have made up my mind to this imam, khalisan lillahi ta’ala, Allahu Akbar.”
