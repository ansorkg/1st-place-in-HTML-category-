```Eid al-Fitr```

Eid al-Fitr (Arabic: عيد الفط) is a religious holiday of Muslims. It is held after the end of the fast. Every year during the month of Ramadan, Muslims over the age of 12 are required to fast for 30 days. The last day of the fast is called "aybashi", and in every house, boorsok is cooked, the Koran is offered to the dead, and alms are given. The next day, the Eid al-Fitr holiday begins. When the sun rises, men put on good clothes, ride horses, and put their boys on decorated donkeys and go to perform the Eid prayer; they pray facing the Qibla. Those who come to the prayer throw money as "prayer money". After the prayer, they divide into groups according to their age (old men, old women, women, men, brides, and children) and go from house to house to celebrate the Eid; they eat and drink from each house and offer the Koran to the dead.
